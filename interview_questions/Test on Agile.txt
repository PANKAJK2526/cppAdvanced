Here are medium-level interview questions on project management methodologies like Agile, specifically tailored for a software engineer role:

---

1. What are the key principles of the Agile Manifesto?
2. How does Agile differ from the Waterfall model in terms of software development?
3. What is the role of a software engineer during a daily stand-up?
4. Can you explain what a Sprint is and how long it typically lasts?
5. How do you estimate effort or time for a user story in Agile?
6. What is the Definition of Done (DoD) and how do you ensure it is met?
7. What tools have you used to manage Agile tasks and track progress?
8. How do you handle changing requirements during an ongoing Sprint?
9. What is a user story? How is it different from a task or a bug?
10. What is backlog grooming (or refinement), and have you participated in it?
11. What is the purpose of a Sprint retrospective? Can you give an example of feedback you gave or received?
12. What is a burn-down chart and what insights can it provide?
13. How do you collaborate with QA/testers in an Agile environment?
14. What is pair programming and when have you used it?
15. Can you explain the difference between Scrum and Kanban?
16. How do you prioritize your tasks when multiple stories are assigned to you?
17. What is technical debt? How do you manage it in Agile development?
18. What challenges have you faced when working in an Agile team, and how did you overcome them?
19. How do you ensure your code is ready for demo or release within the Sprint?
20. What is continuous integration (CI) and how does it fit into Agile?
21. How do Agile ceremonies (planning, review, retrospective) help improve team performance?
22. What is a spike in Agile and when should it be used?
23. How do you deal with blockers in your tasks? How do you communicate them to the team?
24. What is velocity in Agile and how is it calculated?
25. Have you ever contributed to story point estimation? How do you determine the right points?
26. What is the role of a Product Owner and how do you interact with them as an engineer?
